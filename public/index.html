I’m a PhD candidate with the QAECO lab at the University of Melbourne. My research vision is split into three core themes:

  • Conservation biology: what are the priority actions we should take to improve native species persistence?
  • Estimating population dynamics and density: where do species occur, how many individuals are there and how does this change over time?
  • Modelling interactions between species: how does one species impact the spatio-temporal behaviour and population dynamics of another species?

I primarily conduct this research through the lens of predator ecology, invasive animal control and native mammal conservation—guiding optimal management and monitoring protocols for land managers. I firmly beleive in quantitative science which is transparent, reproducible and collaborative.

I pay respect to the traditional custodians of the lands where I live and work - the Wurundjeri, Bundjalung, Gunditjmara, Gadubanud and Kirrae whurrung.
Interests. Predators, Invasive Species, Threat Management, Heirachical Models, Generalised Additive Models, Camera-traps.
